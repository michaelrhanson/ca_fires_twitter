Training exercise caused Cottonwood fire

By Susan Shroder
San Diego Union-Tribune
May 29, 2009 at 3:53 pm

SAN DIEGO — A fire that burned 65 acres of brush and briefly threatened homes in East County last week was accidentally started by federal prison employees who were training at a shooting range, Cal Fire said Friday.

The employees were shooting firearms and using nonlethal weapons designed to distract targets with sound and light, said Karen Banks, public information officer for the Metropolitan Correctional Center in downtown San Diego. The devices are commonly referred to as flash-bang grenades.

The fire broke out about 1 p.m. last Friday behind the Project 2000 gun range on Willow Glen Drive near Singing Canyon Drive in the Cottonwood area and quickly spread in the dry brush, prompting an aggressive air and ground attack involving 300 firefighters.

Cal Fire issued a brief statement Friday that said that the blaze started during the training and that the investigation was continuing.

Banks said two federal prison staff members were burned when they tried to extinguish the blaze after calling 911. The injuries were not life-threatening and they were not hospitalized, she said.

Banks said she did not know exactly which weapons were being fired when the blaze began.

"We're just letting people know that we were conducting training at the range, and it is still unknown what the cause of the fire actually was," Banks said.

She said she did not yet know if the federal government would be liable for firefighting costs.

Six helicopters and five air tankers were initially involved in the firefighting efforts. The blaze was about a mile from the Sycuan Golf Resort, formerly called Singing Hills Country Club.

The fire came within a few hundred yards of some homes, and residents were evacuated. They returned to their homes that night.

One firefighter suffered a heat-related injury.
