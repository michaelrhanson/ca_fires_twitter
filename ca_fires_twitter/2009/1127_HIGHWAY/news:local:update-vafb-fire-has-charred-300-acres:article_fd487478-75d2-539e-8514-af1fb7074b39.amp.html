UPDATE: VAFB fire has charred 300 acres

Staff report
Sep 30, 2009

Flames have consumed at least 300 acres of dense chaparral as a small army of firefighters - working from the ground and the air - attacks a brush fire burning on Vandenberg Air Force Base.

Nearly four hours after the fire?s start, officials reopened most of Highway 1 near the base, although a southbound lane close to the main gate remains shut, the California Highway Patrol reported about 4:30 p.m.

Firefighters battling what has been dubbed the Highway Fire are coping with winds blowing about 20 mph, and gusting to 55 mph.

BAll of our papers have to be anchored down, it?s that windy,C said Capt. David Sadecki with Santa Barbara County Fire Department.

Crews are hoping to hold the fire at Pine Canyon Road - and keep it from crossing Santa Lucia Canyon Road and moving toward Vandenberg Village. A contingency plan is in place to protect the community if the flames move that direction.

The only evacuations issued affect people on the Air Force base.

BWe are evacuating buildings just to be safe,C said Lt. Raymond Geoffroy, a base spokesman.

Buildings being evacuated include those near the southern end of California Boulevard in the area of New Mexico, Utah and Iceland avenues. The affected buildings house the 30th Civil Engineer Squadron plus the 30th Mission Support Group.

In all, about 25 buildings were evacuated, according to Airman 1st Class Angelina Drake. Some of those may include housing, she added.

Additionally, Pine Canyon Road is closed, which means the Lompoc gate (on Pine Canyon) is shut.

A power outage has left Vandenberg in the dark, and all non-mission-essential personnel have been sent home.

The fire started about 11:40 a.m. near Vandenberg Middle School, and jumped Highway 1, heading toward the main area of the base.

The Air Force is leading the fire fight, with assistance from county fire and other departments. The force involved in the battle includes 10 air tankers, six dozers, 20 engines and five helicopters.

Smoke was visible from as far away as the Arroyo Grande area.

Highway 1 near the main gate is closed, with traffic between Santa Maria and Lompoc diverted over Harris Grade Road.

Middle school students and staff were sheltered in place, Geoffroy said, but were prepared to evacuate if necessary.

The cause of the fire, which started near the middle school, is under investigation. Geoffroy initially said the fire started after a transformer exploded, but later said it didn?t appear to be involved.

However, in a press release sent at 3 p.m. officials said the 30th Space Wing emergency response team confirmed the fire broke out due to a blown transformer near the school.
