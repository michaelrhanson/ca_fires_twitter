Stafford Fire threatens Hayfork; crews making progress on north state fires

Firefighters keep the Stafford Fire from crossing Highway 3 on Friday in Hayfork.
Sept. 07, 2012
0 Comments
SHARE

By Sean Longoria and Joe Szydlowski of the Redding Record Searchlight

Sept. 07, 2012 0

STAFFORD FIRE

Location: Southeast of Hayfork, on Wildwood Road

Evacuation center: Solid Rock Church in Hayfork

Origin: Wednesday, human-caused, under investigation

Size: 2,070 acres

Personnel assigned: 1,094

Growth potential: High

Containment: 15 percent; 100 percent expected Wednesday

LIKELY FIRE

Location: East of Highway 395 at Tule Mountain

Origin: Wednesday, fire spread from a motor home fire

Size: 9,675 acres

Personnel assigned: 311

Growth potential: High

Containment: 35 percent; 100 percent expected by Tuesday

BAGLEY FIRE

Location: Shasta-Trinity National Forest land, four miles west of Big Bend

Origin: Lightning, Aug. 18

Size: 45,917 acres

Personnel assigned: 824

Growth potential: Medium

Containment: 80 percent; 100 percent expected by Monday

GOFF FIRE

Location: 10 miles northeast of Happy Camp

Origin: Lightning, Aug. 5

Size: 21,950 acres

Personnel assigned: 1,044

Containment: 67 percent; 100 percent expected Tuesday

CARIBOU FIRE

Location: 15 miles southwest of Coffee Creek, near Snowslide Lake, in Klamath National Forest

Origin: Cause undetermined, discovered Monday

Size: 40 acres

Personnel assigned: 25

Containment: 75 percent; no expected date of full containment given

NORTH PASS FIRE

Location: 10 miles northeast of Covelo in Williams Valley

Origin: Lightning, Aug. 18

Size: 41,983 acres

Personnel assigned: 685

Growth potential: Low

Containment: 76 percent; 100 percent expected Monday by 6 p.m.

HAYFORK ? The Stafford Fire is burning ever-closer to the Trinity County town of Hayfork, prompting officials Friday to close roads and issue additional evacuations.

The 2,070-acre blaze on Friday forced the closure of a stretch of Highway 3 just east of Hayfork, from County Road 343 to Wildwood Road, early Friday afternoon. That stretch of Highway 3 reopened early Friday evening.

Fire officials said the southern and eastern ends of the fire are contained and crews shifted efforts to the southern and western ends. But the fire spat embers north, creating several spot blazes up to half-mile closer to Hayfork, officials said.

Some of those spot fires crossed the highway, though officials said the small fires were quickly extinguished.

Crews are using burnouts to reduce fuels between the active fire and containment lines, officials said.

Evacuations on Morgan Hill Road to Genessee Road, Wildwood Road and Wildthings Road from the north end to Highway 3 remain in effect. The evacuation center is at Solid Rock Church in Hayfork.

At around 4 p.m. Friday, the Trinity County Sheriff's Office issued evacuations for Barker Valley Road along Highway 3 to Big Creek Road and also the mobile home park across from a mill west of Big Creek Road.

National Weather Service forecasters called for warm days and cold nights for Hayfork. Southeast winds up to 6 mph should hit the area today, forecasters said.

Elsewhere, firefighters are reining in two of the north state's larger fires, while a third is proving more challenging than was initially thought.

The Likely Fire in Modoc County grew to some 8,500 acres Friday, just two days after a motor home fire sparked the wildland blaze, fire officials said.

Cool temperatures and high humidity ? normally good firefighting conditions ? overnight Thursday hampered burnout operations, fire officials said.

Crews have kept buildings safe, though 18 homes and seven other buildings are threatened by the blaze.

Firefighters continued Friday to make good progress toward containing the Bagley Fire northeast of Redding.

Officials said the fire, which has burned nearly 46,000 acres, is 80 percent contained, and they expect full containment Monday.

Likewise, the Goff Fire in Siskiyou County is coming under control. The approximately 22,000-acre blaze was 67 percent contained Friday as crews finished contingency lines on the north and west edges of the fire.

"At the outset fire managers have been focusing on full suppression while assuring the safety of the firefighters. The recent increase in containment is the result of the chosen tactics to meet both these objectives," an updated overview of the fire says. "By constructing hand lines directly along the fire's edge firefighters have been able to reduce the size of the fire while minimizing their exposure to hazards."

Officials expect full containment on the fire by Thursday.

Reporter Joe Szydlowski contributed to this story.
