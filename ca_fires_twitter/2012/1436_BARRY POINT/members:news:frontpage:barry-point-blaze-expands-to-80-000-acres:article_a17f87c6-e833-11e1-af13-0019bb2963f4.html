    Barry Point blaze expands to 80,000 acres

    Although the fire is now 30 percent contained, the cost of the effort has added up to $8.5 million

    • By ANDREW CREASEY H&N Staff Reporter

      Holly Owens

      • Author email
    • Aug 17, 2012
    • Aug 17, 2012 Updated Aug 20, 2012

    Two residences 20 miles west of Lakeview were advised to evacuate as the Barry Point fire continued to grow at its northeastern and southern ends.

    The blaze that was started by lightning more than a week ago has grown to almost 80,000 acres.

    The fire stayed within the burn lines established outside Lakeview, but is still growing unimpeded to the south and southeast. It is now 30 percent contained, said Lucinda Nolan, a public information officer with the fire management team.

    The cost of the firefighting effort has reached $8.5 million, according to the National Interagency Coordination Center’s incident management situation report.

    The fire has moved past Crowder Flat Guard Station and Willow Creek Ranch in Modoc County. Structures at the guard station, the ranch and the historic Beaver Dam Cabin were saved thanks to burn lines and water drops that were done on Monday.

    There have been no structures damaged by the fire.

    Firefighters have made progress on the north and northwest flanks. The southern flank will continue to challenge firefighters due to rugged terrain and active fire behavior, but a significant number of resources have been moved to the south as firefighters work to contain that end, according to a fire management team update.

    Hot temperatures, low relative humidity, unstable air and exceedingly dry fuels continue to contribute to large fire growth.

    Relief is on the horizon as far as temperature is concerned, however, with Saturday set to be the last day of 90-degree weather, said Jay Stockton, a senior forecaster with the National Weather Service.

    By Tuesday or Wednesday, the temperature should be in the mid-80s, which is the historic norm for the area, Stockton said.

    A 20 percent chance of thunderstorms that could spark additional fires over the next few days is a cause for concern, Nolan said.

    “We have people watching for it, that’s about all you can do,” Nolan said. “Hopefully we can get an initial attack and put any new fires out.”

    A level 3 evacuation, which advises immediate evacuation, remains in place on the south end of Drews Reservoir and the west shore of Goose Lake from the California-Oregon border south to Forest Road 73.

    A level 1 evacuation, which advises to keep an eye out for fire, remains in effect from the south side of Oregon Highway 140 at Drew’s Gap west of Lakeview then west of Andy Hill Road headed south and continuing south on the west side of the Westside Road in Lake County to the Oregon-California border.
