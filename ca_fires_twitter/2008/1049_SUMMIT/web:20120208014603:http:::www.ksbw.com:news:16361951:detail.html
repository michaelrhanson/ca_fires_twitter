CAL FIRE: Summit Fire 15 Percent Contained

Gov. Declared State Of Emergency For Santa Cruz County

POSTED: 7:21 am PDT May 22, 2008
UPDATED: 10:56 pm PDT May 22, 2008

Email Print
comments
SANTA CRUZ COUNTY, Calif. -- CAL Fire officials said they have been able to contain about 15 percent of the Summit Fire as of 7 p.m.

Gov. Arnold Schwarzenegger declared a state of emergency for Santa Cruz County late Thursday afternoon in response to the massive damage being caused by the Summit Fire.

Fire officials said a wildfire burning in the Santa Cruz Mountains has grown to more than 3,000 acres. The fire is expected to grow to 10,000 acres.

"I want to extend my deepest appreciation to the brave firefighters," Schwarzenegger said. "As I've said many times before, we have the most courageous, talented and best trained firefighters in the world. They are doing a great job fighting the Summit Fire. My administration is working closely with federal and local officials, including Congresswoman Eshoo, Congressman Honda, Senator Maldonado and Assemblymember Laird. We are aggressively battling this fire from the onset. We are putting extra resources on this fire, and we will continue to mobilize and coordinate all the resources necessary to fight it."

About 1,500 homes were evacuated - 336 of them under mandatory orders - as the fire, first reported around 5:30 a.m., continued to grow despite more than 500 firefighters and a swarm of tanker planes and helicopters dousing the area.

Homeowners living on Redwood Road to Hazel Dell Road were ordered to evacuate the area shortly before 3 p.m. Heavy brush and timber and winds gusting up to 50 mph were complicating efforts to fight the blaze. Officials estimated the fire would grow to 10,000 acres before being contained.

"The fuels are very heavy and dry from a pretty mild winter. With that wind added in as a factor, it's a pretty good recipe for fire," said Battalion Chief Mike Marcucci.

Rebecca Henson, 45, was woken up by a neighbor to smoke and ash blanketing her wood cabin in Corralitos. She quickly evacuated with her dog, resigning herself to the possibility that her home burned after they left.

"That thing's gonna go up like a torch - it's got wood floors, wood ceilings, everything," Henson said as she gathered with about 75 other evacuees at a central market in another part of town. "There wasn't an official evacuation, but we're mountain folks and we're pretty used to independent living. So it didn't take too much common sense to realize this thing is close, we gotta go."

The blaze also was circling Maymens Flat, a tiny community of about seven homes that overlooks the Pacific Ocean and the city of Santa Cruz. Kenneth Kim, 66, stood on a ridge overlooking the fire, and peered through binoculars to see how his house was faring against the flames.

Kim initially seemed optimistic. But then the smoke cleared, giving him a better view: Smoke was coming from his home of 20 years.

"Oh, it's gone. It's smoldering," Kim said. "I feel very scared, mad and ... to start all over, I don't know how." New evacuations were ordered for the Lower Highland, Buzzard's Lagoon and Vaca Del Sol areas shortly before 1 p.m.

Monterey Bay Unified Air Pollution District officials said they are tracking the smoke plume and will issue advisories as conditions develop.

The plume passed over Watsonville shortly after noon raining ash down on the area.

People with respiratory ailments, young children and older adults are urged to limit exposure to the smokey air by staying indoors.

"Our main priority is evacuations, getting people out safely," said state fire prevention specialist Chris Morgan. "Then we can start working the perimeter of the fire."

Several structures were destroyed by the flames, and at least three schools in the area closed their doors Thursday because of the fire. No injuries have been reported.

Firefighters from Monterey County, Pacific Grove, Hollister and Fort Hunter-Liggett are helping CAL Fire battle the blaze.

A fire at Old San Jose Road that started this afternoon has been contained.

A fire on Zayante Road near Quail Hollow started about 2:40 p.m., but it is not yet contained.

PG&E officials said some 64 customers in the fire area have had their power shut off on request from firefighters on scene.

Another 1,000 customers in the area are without power. It is unknown if those outages are fire or wind-related.

Evacuation areas have been set up at Mount Madonna County Park, CT English School, the Los Gatos Community Center and the J.J. Crosetti Building at the Santa Cruz County Fairgrounds.

Cots were being set up for overnight stays at the Crosetti Building at the Santa Cruz County Fairgrounds.

Another evacuation center was set up at the Los Gatos Community Center at 208 East Main Street in Los Gatos.

Pajaro Valley Unified School District Superintendent Mary Ann Mays said there were no evacuations from schools in the district. Children are being kept inside due to falling ash.

All Pajaro Valley Unified School District schools will be closed tomorrow.

More than 180 children have been evacuated from Camp Koinonia in the Santa Cruz Mountains to Pajaro Valley High School.

The children, from Cupertino schools, will have buses picking them up in the next few hours.

Summit Road has been closed between Mount Madonna Road and Highway 17. The fire is burning near Highway 17 and Mount Madonna County Park, about ten miles west of Gilroy, or about 40 miles south of San Jose.

CAL Fire officials said they believe the fire started along Summit Road just off Loma Chiquita around 5:30 a.m. They do not yet know what caused the fire to start.

The Red Cross has established a shelter at the Santa Cruz County Fairgrounds.

Anyone that needs to know whether they should evacuate should call CALFIRE at 831-335-5355.

The Santa Cruz County Animal Services Authority assisted with large animal evacuations for most of the day to the Fairgrounds. People needing assistance moving livestock should call 454-7303.

Over 200 horses have been evacuated to the Santa Cruz County Fairgrounds, filling the facility to capacity.

Fairgrounds officials said they are working on finding an alternate facility for more livestock.

Both Santa Cruz County Animal Services Shelters will remain open until 9 p.m. to help with evacuated pets and animals. Both facilities will reopen at 8 a.m. on Friday.

Household pets can be taken to the shelters for temporary housing to displaced residents.

Nisene Marks Park is closed and has been evacuated.

The following roads have been closed:
  • Poleline Road at Summit Drive
  • Mt. Madonna Park Road is open to residents only
  • Eureka Canyon at Hames
  • Browns Valley at Corralitos Road
  • Highlands at Summit
  • Summit Road at Highway 17
  • Summit at San Jose Road
  • Eureka Canyon Road is closed at 4 Corners
  • People are not being allowed to go up Eureka Canyon Road towards the fire.
  • Corralitos at Freedom
  • Varney at Corralitos

The following schools have announced closures
  • Monte Vista Christian will not have classes Friday
  • Aptos High School closed Friday
  • Watsonville Tap
  • Quetal School
  • Luna Park
  • Dewitt Anderson School
  • Watsonville Community School
  • Esperanza Middle School
  • The Success Academy
  • All Pajaro Valley schools closed Friday
