TOWN OF PARADISE
Public Service Announcement - #8
July 8, 2008 – 8:30 pm
DIRECTOR OF EMERGENCY SERVICES
Charles L. Rough, Jr., Town Manager  

This is an evacuation announcement regarding the Camp Fire, which is south of the West Fire and  is currently burning towards the West Branch of the Feather River .

There is an immediate threat evacuation for all residents south of Wagstaff Road to Bille Road between Clark Road and Pentz Road (Portions of Zone 10) and south of Bille Road to the southern Town limits between Sawmill Road and Pentz Road. (Portions of Zones  7 & 10)

There is still an immediate threat evacuation for all residents on the East side of Pentz Road from the Skyway at Pentz Rd. south to the Town limits at Malibu Drive. (Zones 4, 5, 6) Citizens need to immediately evacuate by gathering personal belongings that will fit in your vehicle. Proceed directly to a shelter, public assembly point or alternate location. Please remain calm, use extreme caution when leaving the area, drive safely and follow the instructions of all public safety personnel.

There is a precautionary evacuation in place for all residents on the West side of Pentz Road and east of Clark Road from Pearson Road north to Pentz and Skyway. (Portions of Zones 7 & 10) This includes streets above Wagstaff on both sides of Clark Road in between the Skyway and Pentz Road. There is  also a precautionary evacuation in place for all residents south of Pearson Road between Clark Road and Pentz Roads.   (Zone 7) Residents should prepare to evacuate, gather personal documents and belongings, evacuate large animals and be prepared to leave immediately if conditions change. Maps indicating both the immediate and precautionary evacuation areas are posted on Towns web site at www.townofparadise.com.

There has been a change regarding public assembly points in Paradise. There is an assembly point at the  Tall Pines Entertainment Center, 5445 Clark Road and at the Seventh Day Adventist School at 5699 Academy Drive in Paradise, buses are available for transportation to the shelters.  There are shelters located at the Neighborhood Church in Chico, 2801 Notre Dame Blvd behind Home Depot, Las Plumas High School, 2380 Las Plumas Ave., Oroville, and Oroville Church of the Nazarene, 2238 Monte Vista Avenue.

Butte County has issued precautionary evacuations for the following areas: The Upper Ridge area east of Skyway from lower Coutolenc to DeSabla Reservoir and all cross streets running East of Skyway. Also Coutolenc Road from the Skyway to Lucretia Rd., Old Magalia including Bader Mine Road and the El Dorado Mobile Home Park, the Skyway between Pentz and Coutolenc and the East side of Pentz Road south of Paradise from Malibu to Durham Pentz Road. (Old Magalia Zone & Sawmill Zone)

There has been a loss of 50 homes destroyed in the Concow area.

The Town of Paradise has activated its emergency operations center and is closely monitoring the situation and will be providing updates throughout the day and evening. Information will be updated on the Town of Paradise website at www.townofparadise.com and recorded on the Town’s AM 1500 radio station.  For information you may call the Town of Paradise call center at 872-5951; or Cal Fire at 538-7826 or log onto the Cal Fire website at www.fire.ca.gov. We encourage residents to stay tuned for updates and monitor local information.

Frankie Rutledge, Vice Mayor
Acting Public Information Officer

Fire on the Ridge Community Meeting
Thursday, July 10th, CMA Church, 6491 Clark Road, Paradise

This meeting is hosted by the Paradise and Butte County Fire Safe Council and the Town of Paradise to give information regarding preparing yourself and your household to survive a wildfire. Come learn the recipe of success for living with wildfire from local experts in this community event to be held this Thursday, July 10th, 2008, 6:30PM to 9:00PM at the CMA Church, 6491 Clark Road in Paradise.

Learn how to be ready when wildfire strikes. You can make a difference for your family, home and community! During the seminar video and photo presentations will provide information on:

Family and personal preparedness tips

Animal wildfire preparedness

Ordering reflective address signs

Find out how to keep brush from growing back

Community wildfire safety projects

Removal of invasive Scotch Broom and

Forest Stewardship

SBA Offers Disaster Assistance to California
Residents and Businesses Affected by the Humboldt and Ophir Fires
SACRAMENTO – Jovita Carranza, Acting Administrator of the U. S. Small Business Administration (SBA), announced that low-interest disaster loans are now available to residents and businesses affected by the Humboldt and Ophir Fires that began June 10 and 11 in Butte County.
Click here for more information.

FEDERAL DISASTER ASSISTANCE
Federal Disaster Loans for Homeowners, Renters and Businesses of All Sizes
The U. S. Small Business Administration (SBA) is offering low-interest disaster loans to homeowners, renters, and non-farm businesses of all sizes who suffered losses caused by the Butte County – Humboldt & Ophir Fires that began on June 10 and June 11 2008.
Click here for more information.

U.S. SMALL BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION
DISASTER LOANS - FACT SHEET
Whether you rent or own your own home, own your own business, or own a small agricultural cooperative located in a declared disaster area, and are the victim of a disaster, you may be eligible for financial assistance from the U. S. Small Business Administration (SBA).
