Comanche Fire update provided

  • By Tehachapi News Staff
  • Sep 12, 2011
  • Sep 12, 2011 Updated Sep 1, 2016
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

Firefighters will be busy today with the Comanche Fire as warmer temperatures and light winds are expected, along with the possibility of more thunderstorms.

In an information release at 8 a.m., Kern County Fire Dept. announced that the other fires in the Comanche complex -- the Knob, Wolf and Harris fires -- are 100 percent contained.

Fire activity increased yesterday afternoon on the southeast portion of the fire as it moved up to the Stallion Springs area. Overnight, firefighters worked to protect homes in the area. Despite the increased fire activity, no structures were destroyed.

A recommended evacuation order remains in effect for the Stallion Springs area. An evacuation center is open at Jacobsen Junior High School, 711 Anita, Tehachapi (entrance off Snyder by the swimming pool).

Road Closures: Comanche Point Road at Stallion Springs Drive. Additionally, it has been reported that a "hard closure" is in place on Banducci Road at Stallion Springs. Anyone who leaves Stallion will not be allowed back in unless they have ID and a blue Stallion Springs sticker; no one will be allowed west of Horsethief Drive.

As of the 8 a.m. report, the fire had covered 23,100 acres and was 30 percent contained. An estimated 2,300 structures are threatened. Additional resources have been brought to the fire with structure protection and line-building during the night and aerial attack to begin this morning. A total of 1152 firefighters are on the fire including 90 fire engines, 35 cire crews, 27 bulldozers, six air tankers, eight helicopters and thre water tenders.

Cooperating Agencies: CAL FIRE, Kern County Fire Department, Stallion Springs Police Department, Bear Valley Police Department, California Highway Patrol, American Red Cross, CalEMA, So Cal Edison, Tejon Ranch, Department of Water Resources, Kern County Sheriff’s Department.

Additional Information: Kern County residents can call 211 for general information.

Additional updates will be posted as information is available. Also check www.bakersfield.com and the Tehachapi News facebook page.
